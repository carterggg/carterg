BOLTZ ON BOARD



WHAT?
Recycled Skateboard Jewelry

ROLE?
Co-owner, Graphic and Web Designer, and Jewelry Creator.

WHY?
For the love of skateboarding, the ability to recycle what I love and wear it around my finger.

How?
Was in high school, skating a lot, started working with a friend creating graphics for his appeal company and I ran into this cleaned up surfer-looking man, name was Mark "Boltz" Boltwood. Mark helped my friend’s company advertise itself and also helped get connections through out the industy. He was around a lot so we ended up skateboarding a lot together; Mark ended up being a jewelry making fanatic with a dream to create hand made jewelry out of broken and retired skateboards. So me being me, an eighteen year old kid with a weird mind and a dream of giving my creative skills an adventure and hope of success, I pushed him to succeed and turn his idea into what is now Boltz on Board.



Design Role:
I do all of the graphic and web design work for the company, including tee shirts, stickers, banners, ads, you know, you name it. The largest percent of graphic work that I do is for custom engraving which we offer on almost all of our products, I create the design that the customer wants in a ‘clean’ 300 DPI black and white formatted vector image. From there we bring it to the lab, measure everything out, position the product perfectly, and engrave it. Never thought that engraving into the skateboard “plyz” would be one of the coolest things to look at.


*KRUZ custom engraving on a four fingered ring.

Jewelry Creator:
2K15, It is Mark Boltwood, myself, and a few other jewelry designers creating Boltz on Board jewelry. Mark has taught me everything I know about it and now it is just a weird cool skill I have, to create jewelry out of wood. I have skills in pretty much every wood designing tool from table saws to drummels, all the way to basic sand paper.
